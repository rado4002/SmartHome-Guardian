## Stakeholder Analysis

| Stakeholder | Role / Description | Interest Level | Influence / Power | Key Needs / Concerns | Priority for Communication |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Home Owner (Primary Actor)** | Urban/semi-urban DRC homeowner using smart devices for security and energy monitoring | High | Medium (adoption drives value) | Reliable battery alerts, easy remote access, security in high-risk areas, low cost and lightweight application | High – demos and feedback loops |
| **Administrator** | Platform overseer responsible for user/device management and system monitoring | High | High (access and rules control) | Audit trails, role-based security, system reliability, intuitive admin dashboard | High – early validation |
| **Technician** | Installs and troubleshoots devices (limited availability in the context) | Medium | Medium (maintenance impact) | Clear alerts and diagnostics, remote insights to reduce physical site visits | Medium – targeted features |
| **Simulated IoT Device** | External boundary actor (Smart Lock, Motion Sensor, Smart Plug) | Low | Low (simulated component) | N/A – behavior handled through simulation | None |
| **Future SaaS Provider / Partners** | Potential ecosystem partners for future smart device integrations | Low | Low (outside MVP scope) | Long-term scalability and maintainability | Low |

---

## Stakeholder Communication Strategy

| Strategy | Stakeholders | Action Plan |
|---|---|---|
| **Manage Closely** |  Administrator | Weekly meetings and strict adherence to UML and SRS standards |
| **Keep Satisfied** | Homeowner | Focus on mobile app usability and reliable battery alerts |
| **Keep Informed** | Technician | Ensure system logs errors clearly and provide diagnostic information |
| **Monitor** | SaaS Partners | Maintain clean architecture so future integrations remain possible |
